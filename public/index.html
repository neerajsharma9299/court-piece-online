      How to Play

      Court Piece is a 4-player trick-taking game, played in two fixed teams: you and the player across from you versus the other two.

      Trump & Challenge

      Each round, the dealer's left-hand player picks the trump suit. Going around the table, every other player then gets one chance to change the trump instead -- the dealer gets the final say. If someone changes it, their team must win all 13 tricks to score; if the other team wins two tricks in a row, the challenge fails instantly.

      Tricks & Capturing

      Follow the suit that was led if you can. Highest card of the led suit wins the trick -- unless someone plays trump, which beats everything. Tricks don't score immediately: they pile up until one player wins two in a row, at which point they claim the whole pile. The 13th trick always goes to whoever wins it, streak or not.

      Winning a Round

      In a normal round, the trump-holding team needs 8 of the 13 tricks. Win all 13 and it's a "court" -- an automatic point. First team to 5 round points wins the match.
